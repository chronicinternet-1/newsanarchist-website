The Take
Marcus Webb · Surveillance & Tech Privacy

The church nursery check-in system is a textbook case of security theater masquerading as child safety. I've reviewed dozens of these implementations—QR codes, RFID wristbands, digital sign-out logs—and they consistently solve zero actual problems while creating substantial data architecture risks. Here's what's actually happening: your congregation just became a dataset. Every pickup time, caregiver pattern, child demographic gets logged into a third-party server (usually poorly secured SaaS infrastructure). That's vulnerability surface. That's liability. The real issue these systems supposedly address—unauthorized child pickups—was already handled by human verification. Staff knew the kids. They asked questions. It worked. What changed? The vendor convinced your admin team that digitization equals competence. I've worked the other side. I know how this infrastructure gets accessed, subpoenaed, breached. The operational security theater of a tech-mediated system is worse than the analog friction it replaced. Push back. Ask your church what happens to that data. Ask where servers sit. Ask what happens in a breach. Demand they document it. They won't have answers—that's the real problem.
