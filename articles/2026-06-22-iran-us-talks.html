Iran-US Talks

Lifting sanctions on Iranian oil was among the subjects pursued during negotiations between Iran and the United States

Elena Vasquez
Former foreign correspondent, 15 years covering conflict zones
June 22, 2026
4 min read
AI-ASSISTED

The first round of US-Iran talks concluded in Switzerland, with mediators stating that lifting sanctions on Iranian oil was among the subjects seriously pursued during negotiations, as reported by Hamid Bovard, managing director of the National Iranian Oil Company, who spoke to Iranian news outlets. The talks aimed to address the ongoing tensions between the two countries, with the US seeking to pressure Iran into reaching a deal, while also reducing its use of military force in the conflict, as part of the Trump Administration's "Economic Fury" sanctions campaign against Iran. This campaign, which lasted for two months, aimed to coerce the Iranian government into negotiations, and its outcome is still being assessed, with some analysts arguing that it has not terminated the main threats in Iran, as discussed in recent reports.

The US-Iran deal, which was signed virtually, reopened the Strait of Hormuz, established a framework for de-escalation, and launched a 60-day process aimed at negotiating a broader accord covering sanctions, security issues, and Iran's nuclear program, as outlined in news updates. The agreement also includes the lifting of the American naval blockade, a $300 billion reconstruction fund to be created by the Gulf Arab states, the release of billions of dollars in frozen assets, and an end to all American sanctions, according to reports. Meanwhile, around 6 million barrels of Iranian crude are onboard three US-sanctioned supertankers that have entered the chokepoint, as tracked by ship-tracking data.

As the situation in the region continues to unfold, analysts are watching closely to see how the US-Iran deal will impact the broader geopolitical landscape, including the role of other major powers, such as China, which is expanding its influence in the region. The conflict has also drawn in other countries, including Israel, which has continued to strike south Lebanon despite a ceasefire, resulting in the deaths of 16 people, as reported by news outlets. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky has also been active in the region, targeting the occupiers' military logistics, oil industry, and air defense in Russian-annexed Crimea, as stated on social media.

The Elena Vasquez Take
Global Power & Geopolitics

As I reflect on the Iran-US talks, I firmly believe that a failure to reach a mutually beneficial agreement will only serve to embolden hardline elements in both countries. My thesis is that the status quo of hostility and mistrust is unsustainable and ultimately detrimental to the interests of both nations. If nothing changes, I argue that the Iranian regime wins, as it can continue to use the specter of American aggression to justify its authoritarian grip on power and distract from its own internal problems, while the US and its allies are left to grapple with the consequences of a potentially nuclear-armed Iran.
