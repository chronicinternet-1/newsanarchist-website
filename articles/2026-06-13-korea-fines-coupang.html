South Korea fines e-commerce giant Coupang $409 million over data leak

Marcus Webb AI-ASSISTED June 12, 2026 4 min read
ORIGINAL REPORTING

In a significant move to hold corporations accountable for data breaches, the South Korean government has announced that it will fine e-commerce giant Coupang a staggering 625 billion won, equivalent to $409.30 million, for its role in a massive leak of customer information last year. This penalty, the largest of its kind in the country, comes as a result of Coupang's alleged illegal collection of personal information, which has raised serious concerns about the company's commitment to protecting the privacy of its users.

The data breach, which occurred in 2022, has been linked to a suspected Russian hacker who is currently in US custody after being extradited from Thailand. The individual has been charged with facilitating a campaign of cyberattacks carried out by a Russia-aligned group that targeted numerous US companies. The fact that a foreign entity was involved in the breach highlights the global nature of cybersecurity threats and the need for companies to prioritize data protection.

The fine imposed on Coupang serves as a reminder that governments are taking a tougher stance on data privacy violations. In recent months, countries such as Australia and Canada have introduced legislation aimed at protecting the online privacy of citizens, particularly children. For instance, the Canadian government introduced a digital safety bill on Wednesday that would ban social media for children under 16, with exemptions for platforms that meet certain safety standards. Similarly, the Digital Personal Data Protection Bill, 2023, which is currently being tracked, recognizes the importance of protecting individual privacy and seeks to regulate the processing of personal data by businesses and government entities.

The Coupang fine is a significant development in the ongoing debate about data privacy and corporate accountability. As a former NSA contractor turned privacy watchdog, I believe that this penalty sends a strong message to companies that they must prioritize the protection of user data. The fact that Coupang is facing a fine of $409.30 million demonstrates that governments are willing to take action against companies that fail to meet their data protection obligations. As we move forward, it is essential that companies take a proactive approach to data protection, and that governments continue to hold them accountable for any violations.
