    IMDB account creation

    I was logged into amazon, and browsing IMDB reading about some artists. Shortly there after I received an email to the alias I only use for amazon welcoming me to IMDB. Amazon is auto creating accounts on IMDB. I found some others that this has happened to.
    Marcus Webb
    Former intelligence contractor
    June 9, 2026
    3 min read
    Share

    What they're not telling you: Amazon Is Automatically Creating IMDB Accounts Without User Consent Amazon is automatically generating IMDB user accounts tied to email addresses of customers who have never signed up for the service, using credential data harvested from Amazon's primary login infrastructure. Multiple users have reported receiving IMDB welcome emails at email addresses they use exclusively for Amazon accounts—addresses they never submitted to IMDB directly. The pattern indicates Amazon is deploying automated account creation at IMDB, a subsidiary Amazon acquired in 1998, by transferring customer email identifiers across its internal systems without explicit user authorization or notification prior to account generation.

    What the Documents Show

    The mechanism appears to operate through Amazon's unified login infrastructure. When a logged-in Amazon user browses IMDB, Amazon's backend systems detect the session and generate a corresponding IMDB account using the Amazon account's registered email address. Users discover the account only after receiving welcome notifications from IMDB—a notification that serves as evidence of account creation rather than a request for permission. This practice differs meaningfully from standard opt-in account creation. Platforms typically require users to submit an email address, create a password, or explicitly confirm account setup before sending welcome communications.

    🔎 Mainstream angle
    The corporate press either ignored this story entirely or buried it in a 3-sentence brief. The framing, when it appeared at all, focused on process rather than impact.

    Follow the Money

    Amazon has inverted that sequence: the account exists first, the user learns about it second. This structure ensures minimal friction in expanding IMDB's registered user base while maximizing the volume of email addresses and associated behavioral data tied to active accounts. The technical infrastructure enabling this process relies on Amazon's identity management systems, which maintain comprehensive records of customer email addresses, browsing behavior, and session activity. By cross-linking Amazon login sessions with IMDB account generation, Amazon can attribute IMDB activity—viewing history, ratings, searches, watchlist activity—directly to authenticated Amazon customer profiles. This consolidation increases the granularity of behavioral data Amazon holds on individual users across its entertainment division. What makes this noteworthy for data collection purposes is the scope.

    What Else We Know

    Amazon's customer base exceeds 200 million active accounts globally. If Amazon is systematically auto-generating IMDB accounts for users who browse IMDB while logged into Amazon, the company is expanding IMDB's registered user count without requiring users to take affirmative action. This inflates IMDB's apparent user engagement metrics while guaranteeing that behavioral data generated on IMDB—film preferences, search queries, ratings patterns—flows automatically into Amazon's consolidated customer intelligence systems. Amazon has not publicly disclosed this auto-account-creation practice. IMDB's terms of service do not explicitly address automatic account provisioning through Amazon credential systems. The company has not released documentation explaining under what circumstances, or for what percentage of its customer base, this automated process triggers.
