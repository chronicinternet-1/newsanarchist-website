Broadview Six Seek Justice

Now-cleared protesters request special prosecutor over alleged prosecutorial misconduct

The Broadview Six protesters, who were recently cleared of charges, are seeking justice and requesting a special prosecutor to investigate government lawyers involved in their case. The request was made to U.S. District Judge April Perry, who discovered apparent prosecutorial misconduct after reviewing unredacted transcripts of the grand jury proceedings. The misconduct included the dismissal of at least one grand juror by Assistant U.S. Attorney Sheri Mecklenburg, as well as inappropriate conversations with two other grand jurors outside the grand jury room.

The case against the Broadview Six imploded in dramatic fashion last month, with Judge Perry discovering the prosecutorial misconduct in late May. The protesters' lawyers are now trying to uncover communication between the White House and the Chicago U.S. Attorney's Office regarding the misconduct. They are also seeking to investigate potential dealings between the two entities, which could have led to the alleged misconduct. The request for a special prosecutor is based on the principle that "no one can credibly investigate themselves," and that an independent investigation is necessary to ensure justice is served.

The Broadview Six case has drawn attention to the issue of prosecutorial misconduct and the need for accountability in the justice system. The case has also raised questions about the involvement of the Trump administration's Justice Department in the prosecution. The protesters' lawyers are seeking to probe the office of U.S. Attorney John Lausch, as well as the Trump administration's potential role in the case. The investigation could potentially lead to criminal contempt charges against government lawyers involved in the case.

Judge Perry, who has experience handling criminal contempt cases, is being asked to appoint a special prosecutor to investigate the allegations of misconduct. The protesters' lawyers are arguing that an independent investigation is necessary to ensure that those responsible for the misconduct are held accountable. The case is ongoing, and it remains to be seen whether a special prosecutor will be appointed and what the outcome of the investigation will be. The Broadview Six protesters are seeking justice and closure, and are hoping that the investigation will lead to a full accounting of the events that led to their prosecution.

The request for a special prosecutor is a significant development in the case, and highlights the need for transparency and accountability in the justice system. The case has already led to the dismissal of the charges against the Broadview Six, and could potentially lead to further consequences for those involved in the alleged misconduct. As the investigation continues, it is likely that more details will emerge about the events surrounding the case, and the role of government lawyers and the Trump administration's Justice Department.
