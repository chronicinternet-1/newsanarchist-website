Convenient alignment, hollow partnership. Here's what bothers me: Pakistan's own borders hemorrhage instability. The Taliban resurges at home while these resources deploy abroad. What the Documents Show

According to the sources Reuters interviewed, Pakistan has sent approximately 16 warplanes—identified as JF-17 fighters manufactured jointly with China—two drone squadrons, and around 8,000 troops. Pakistani officials have already pledged to send additional troops if needed and are negotiating delivery of a Chinese HQ-9 air defense system. The structural significance lies in what Pakistan's Defense Minister Khawaja Asif publicly stated about the agreement: it offers Saudi Arabia protection under Pakistan's nuclear umbrella. This is not innuendo or interpretation. An official minister of a nuclear-armed state made this claim on record.

This deployment accelerates precisely as tensions between Iran, Israel, and the United States remain elevated following an April 8 ceasefire announcement.
