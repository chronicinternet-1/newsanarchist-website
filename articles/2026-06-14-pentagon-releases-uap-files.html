Pentagon Releases UAP Files

Pentagon releases third batch of formerly classified UFO documents amid criticism

Casey North AI-ASSISTED June 14, 2026 4 min read

The Pentagon has released its third batch of formerly classified UFO documents, shedding more light on the phenomenon of Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena, or UAPs. This latest tranche of documents, released on June 12, 2026, includes never-before-released accounts and artist renderings of UAPs, and has sparked renewed interest in the topic. According to the Pentagon's UAP website, the site has received over 1.7 billion hits since May, a staggering number that underscores the public's fascination with the subject.

The release of these documents has been met with a mix of excitement and skepticism, with some hailing it as a major breakthrough in the quest for transparency and others criticizing the government for not releasing more information sooner. Executive Director Jordan Flowers of the Disclosure Foundation, a non-profit organization advocating for UAP transparency, has been at the forefront of the effort to push for greater disclosure. In a recent interview, Flowers discussed the significance of the newly released documents, including a senior intelligence officer's firsthand encounter with a UAP and newly declassified documents related to nuclear facilities.

The timing of the release is also noteworthy, coinciding with the release of Steven Spielberg's new film "Disclosure Day", which explores the theme of UFO disclosure. As Ross Coulthart, a journalist with NewsNation, noted in a recent special report, the events leading up to the US government's openness about UAPs are complex and multifaceted. Avi Loeb, a prominent scientist and advocate for UAP research, has also weighed in on the issue, calling for a more scientific approach to the study of UAPs and the establishment of a UAP Science Advisory Council to guide the US government's efforts.

Despite the progress made in recent months, critics argue that the government is still not releasing enough information, and that the documents released so far are heavily redacted and lacking in detail. The Department of Defense has confirmed that additional tranches of UAP-related documents will be released on a rolling basis throughout 2026, which is expected to continue fueling the debate between official government assessments and independent researchers. As the public continues to demand more information and transparency, it remains to be seen how the government will respond to these calls and what further revelations will come to light.
