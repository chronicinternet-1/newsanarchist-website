FOIA Reform Stalled, Advocates Point to Lack of Transparency

The Freedom of Information Act reform efforts have stalled in Congress, leaving advocates for government transparency frustrated and concerned. Despite repeated calls for action, lawmakers have failed to make significant progress on the issue. The FOIA reform bill, which aims to increase access to government records and reduce secrecy, has been stuck in committee for months. ProPublica, a nonprofit newsroom, has been at the forefront of the push for FOIA reform, using donations from individuals to fund investigations into corporate and government wrongdoing.

According to reports, the White House has been slow to respond to FOIA requests, with some requests taking years to process. The cost of these delays is substantial, with estimates suggesting that the government spends millions of dollars each year responding to FOIA lawsuits. In 2020, the government spent over $500 million on FOIA-related litigation, a figure that has been steadily increasing in recent years. Frank Peavey, a witness in a recent congressional hearing, highlighted the need for greater transparency and oversight, citing concerns over immigration enforcement and congressional oversight of ICE policies.

The House of Representatives has held several hearings on the issue, including a recent hearing on June 15, 2026, where lawmakers discussed the need for greater transparency and accountability. However, despite these efforts, the FOIA reform bill remains stalled. Advocates point to the lack of transparency and accountability in the current system, citing cases where the government has withheld information from the public, often at great expense. For example, a recent CBS News 8 investigation found that the government had spent over $100,000 on a single FOIA lawsuit, highlighting the need for reform.

The White House has come under criticism for its handling of FOIA requests, with some advocates accusing the administration of deliberately stalling or withholding information. The White House Wire, a website that provides updates on White House news and policies, has been silent on the issue, fueling concerns that the administration is not taking the issue seriously. As the stalemate continues, advocates for government transparency are growing increasingly frustrated, pointing to the need for greater accountability and oversight. With the cost of FOIA-related litigation continuing to rise, it remains to be seen whether lawmakers will take action to address the issue.

As I reflect on the current state of FOIA reform, I firmly believe that stagnation will only serve to undermine government transparency. If nothing changes, it's clear that government agencies will be the winners, as they will continue to withhold information from the public with relative ease. I argue that meaningful reform is necessary to ensure that citizens have access to the information they deserve. Without it, agencies will remain opaque, and the public will be left in the dark. It's imperative that we push for reform to hold our government accountable and bring about a new era of transparency.
