What Else We Know

While genealogical databases like GEDmatch have proven instrumental in solving violent crimes, they operate outside traditional warrant frameworks. Users voluntarily uploaded DNA, but law enforcement's subsequent searches of that data without explicit consent from database owners remains legally ambiguous. The Colonial Parkway resolution may have been achieved through methods that wouldn't have been permissible a decade ago, raising the question: how many other solved cases quietly relied on forensic strategies that would face constitutional challenge if properly litigated? The broader implication cuts deeper than one case.
