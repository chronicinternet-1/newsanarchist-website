SEC Removes Barrier

SEC proposes to rescind Rule 611, unlocking blockchain-based trading

Casey North AI-ASSISTED June 14, 2026 4 min read

The US Securities and Exchange Commission has taken a significant step towards embracing blockchain-based trading, with a proposal to rescind two decades-old rules that have hindered the growth of this technology. On June 11, 2026, the SEC submitted an official proposal to remove Rule 611 and Rule 610(e) of Regulation NMS, a move that could have far-reaching implications for the crypto industry. This decision is part of the SEC's broader Project Crypto initiative, launched in August, which aims to provide clarity and guidance on the regulation of digital assets.

The removal of these rules could be a major unlock for crypto tokenized US stocks, allowing for greater efficiency and transparency in trading. This development has been welcomed by many in the industry, who see it as a significant step towards mainstream adoption of blockchain technology. Senator Lummis, a long-time advocate for crypto regulation, has also been pushing for clarity on the classification of digital assets, with her proposed CLARITY Act aiming to create a clear jurisdictional boundary between the SEC and the Commodity Futures Trading Commission.

However, not everyone is convinced that this move is a positive one. Some lawmakers, including Senator Cortez Masto, have expressed concerns that the CLARITY Act could undermine law enforcement's ability to trace illicit finance and recover victims' money. The Act's definition of a money transmitter has been criticized for being too narrow, potentially leaving developers and other industry players outside of the regulatory framework. As the debate around crypto regulation continues to evolve, it will be interesting to see how these developments play out in the coming months.

The SEC's proposal to remove Rule 611 and Rule 610(e) is a significant development, but it is just one part of a larger conversation around crypto regulation. With the CLARITY Act still making its way through the legislative process, it remains to be seen how these changes will ultimately impact the industry. One thing is certain, however: the crypto landscape is changing rapidly, and regulators are finally starting to take notice. As of June 12, 2026, the crypto market is waiting with bated breath to see how these developments will play out, with many industry players eagerly anticipating the potential benefits of a more streamlined regulatory framework.
