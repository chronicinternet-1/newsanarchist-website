Russia Refurbishes Military

Russia has refurbished thousands of vehicles and artillery pieces in recent years, increasing its military power

Elena Vasquez
Former foreign correspondent, 15 years covering conflict zones
June 24, 2026
4 min read
AI-ASSISTED

Russia's military refurbishment efforts have been underway for several years, with the country pulling thousands of vehicles and artillery pieces from its stocks and refurbishing or newly producing thousands of vehicles. According to a report by Foreign Affairs, the Russian military has likely increased its number of armored combat vehicles, including tanks, to levels comparable to or slightly higher than those at the start of the war in Ukraine. This development has significant implications for the global balance of power, particularly in the context of economic sanctions imposed on Russia by Western nations.

The Kremlin's ability to adapt to these sanctions has been a key factor in its military refurbishment efforts, with Russia seeking to reduce its dependence on Western technology and supplies. As noted in a recent article on Global Energy Shifts, Russia's energy exports have played a crucial role in its ability to weather the economic sanctions. Meanwhile, the United States and its allies have been increasing their military presence in the region, with NATO Secretary General Mark Rutte calling for increased weapons stocks on both sides of the Atlantic, as reported by eu.news-pravda.com.

The Russian military's refurbishment efforts have also been aided by supplies from other countries, including North Korea, which has provided over 300 pieces of artillery. This development has significant implications for the regional balance of power, particularly in the context of the ongoing conflict in Ukraine. As Senator Marco Rubio's recent visit to the UAE highlights, the United States is seeking to strengthen its alliances in the region, as discussed in the article Rubio Visits UAE. Furthermore, China's decision to Tighten Indium Export controls has also had a significant impact on the global supply chain, with potential implications for Russia's military refurbishment efforts.

The Elena Vasquez Take
Global Power & Geopolitics

As I reflect on Russia's efforts to refurbish its military, I firmly believe that this move poses a significant threat to global stability. My thesis is that if left unchecked, Russia's military resurgence will embolden its aggressive expansionism. I fear that if nothing changes, Russia will be the clear winner, gaining increased influence and control over neighboring regions. The international community must take a strong stance against Russia's militarization to prevent this outcome. If we fail to act, Russia's gains will come at the expense of its neighbors and the world will become a more volatile and dangerous place.
