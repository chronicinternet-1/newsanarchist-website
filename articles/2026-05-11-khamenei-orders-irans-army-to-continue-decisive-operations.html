The order came during a high-level meeting between Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei and Major General Ali Abdollahi, commander of the Iranian army's Khatam al-Anbiya Headquarters. According to Iran's state broadcaster IRIB, Khamenei issued "new directives and guidance for continuing operations and confronting enemies decisively." Abdollahi presented a readiness report on Iran's armed forces during the same meeting, indicating coordinated military planning at the highest levels of the Iranian government.

What the Documents Show

The timing of this announcement carries particular significance given months of Western speculation about Khamenei's health and whereabouts. The Guardian and The Times had published reports—sourced to unnamed intelligence officials—claiming the Supreme Leader was in a coma or had fled to Russia following alleged US-Israeli strikes. These claims created a narrative vacuum about Iran's actual leadership structure and decision-making capacity. Mazaher Hosseini, head of protocol in Khamenei's office, recently addressed the rumors directly, stating that Khamenei sustained only "minor injuries" and is now "in complete health." He also suggested Khamenei would address the public "when the time is right," implying controlled information management around the Supreme Leader's status. The Iranian order contradicts the mainstream Western framing that portrayed Khamenei as incapacitated or removed from power.

Just days before the military directive, CNN cited US intelligence sources claiming Khamenei "is playing a critical role in shaping war strategy alongside senior Iranian officials." This intelligence assessment aligns with the IRIB announcement, suggesting Khamenei remains operationally engaged in military decision-making. Additionally, Iran's President Masoud Pezeshkian met with the Supreme Leader recently and described being impressed by his "vision and humble and sincere approach," further contradicting narratives of diminished capacity. What the mainstream press has underplayed is the distinction between Western speculation about Khamenei's incapacity and evidence of his actual operational control.
