Trump Retaliation Tracker

The Trump administration's use of arrests, prosecutions, and investigations as retaliation is being tracked

The Trump administration's use of retaliatory actions against perceived enemies has been a subject of concern, with many instances of arrests, prosecutions, and investigations being tracked by organizations such as Protect Democracy, which has documented numerous cases of retaliation against high-level federal employees. One notable example is the criminal investigation of a federal employee for actions taken in the scope of their office, which resulted in prosecutions and convictions, and was widely seen as an unusual and potentially retaliatory move. This investigation was one of several initiated by the Trump administration, including two separate investigations into the conduct of the 2016 Russia investigation, one by the DOJ inspector general and one by Special Counsel John Durham.

The use of retaliatory actions has not been limited to the Trump administration's political enemies, but has also been seen in other areas, such as the Department of Defense, where a heavily redacted report titled "Audit of the Acquisition of the Long Range Strike Bomber" was obtained through the Freedom of Information Act, as reported by twz.com, and revealed concerns about the acquisition process for the new B-21 Raiders. Meanwhile, the FBI and other agencies are investigating allegations of wrongdoing, including a case in which the Harris County District Attorney's Office is seeking access to federal investigation documents, as reported by lisaremillard.substack.com.

The issue of retaliation has also been raised in the context of the Chao Ethics Probe, which has highlighted concerns about the use of government power to target perceived enemies. Similarly, the DHS IG Review Hindered has raised questions about the ability of government agencies to conduct independent investigations without interference from political appointees. Furthermore, the Schiff Probes DOJ has shed light on the need for congressional oversight of the executive branch's use of power. As the investigation into the new Air Force One continues, with subpoenas being issued and reporters being compelled to testify, it remains to be seen how these issues will be resolved, and what implications they will have for the use of government power in the future.
