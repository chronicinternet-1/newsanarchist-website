Crypto Hacks Surge

DeFi loses $840 million to hackers this year

Casey North AI-ASSISTED June 14, 2026 4 min read

The world of cryptocurrency has been plagued by a surge in hacks, with over $840 million stolen so far this year, primarily from decentralized finance, or DeFi, platforms. This staggering figure has left many in the industry on high alert, wondering what can be done to prevent such brazen attacks. According to reports from CoinDesk, the rise of powerful cyber tools, such as Anthropic's Claude Fable 5, has put DeFi platforms at an increased risk of being hacked, with some experts warning that the next billion-dollar hacker may be able to move at superhuman speed.

One of the most recent high-profile hacks was the theft of $36 million from the Humanity Protocol, which the company has blamed on North Korea-linked hackers. This incident highlights the need for increased security measures in the crypto industry, particularly in the DeFi sector. As reported by Crypto News, other companies, such as KuCoin, are also facing scrutiny over unpaid judgments, with one investor citing a $2 million unpaid judgment from a Seychelles court.

The Block has reported on various incidents of hacks and thefts in the crypto space, including the use of AI-powered tools to hijack private messaging apps. Decrypt has also warned that hackers can use simple sentences to hijack popular AI chatbots like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. With the rise of Bitcoin Finance, or BTCFi, and the growing ecosystem of decentralized financial applications being built on or around Bitcoin, the need for robust security measures has never been more pressing.

As the crypto market continues to evolve, with new protocols and innovations emerging daily, it is essential that companies prioritize security and take steps to protect their users' assets. The latest news and updates from the crypto world, as reported by CNBC Crypto World, highlight the importance of staying informed about key innovations and market moves. With the surge in crypto hacks showing no signs of slowing down, it is crucial that the industry takes a proactive approach to security, or risk losing the trust of investors and users alike.
