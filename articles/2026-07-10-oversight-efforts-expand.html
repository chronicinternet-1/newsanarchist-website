Oversight Efforts Expand

US government oversight entities unite to deliver independent oversight of assistance to Ukraine

Jordan Calloway
Investigative journalist specializing in FOIA litigation
July 10, 2026
4 min read
AI-ASSISTED

Oversight efforts are expanding as the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) turns 60, with many calling for increased government transparency, as seen in the recent article FOIA Turns 60 Amid Blockage, which highlights the challenges faced by those seeking information. The landmark law has helped reveal critical information about various government activities, including the Vietnam War, and has been instrumental in holding those in power accountable. However, despite its importance, the federal government is blocking more documents than ever, prompting concerns about the erosion of transparency.

The House's investigation into Jeffrey Epstein is a prime example of the need for oversight, with Committee Democrats seeking to subpoena testimony from FBI Director Kash Patel, as reported by politico.com. The investigation has also raised questions about the federal government's handling of the matter, with some witnesses being called back to give formal depositions. This renewed focus on oversight is also reflected in the work of Oversight.gov, which has brought together over 20 oversight entities to deliver independent, whole-of-government oversight of U.S. assistance to Ukraine.

Transparency is essential for accountability, and government agencies must be willing to provide information to the public, as argued in the article Oversight Expands. The Canadian Association of Journalists recently awarded the Government of Alberta the 2026 "Code of Silence" Award for Government Secrecy, highlighting the need for greater transparency. In the United States, the U.S. Government Publishing Office provides access to government documents, including the Budget of the U.S. Government, through GovInfo. Chester Receiver Vijay Kapoor has also emphasized that government transparency should not depend on "which side you're on," but rather on a commitment to accountability, as seen in the article Congress Probes IG Failures.

As the Freedom of Information Act turns 60, it is clear that there is still much work to be done to ensure government transparency, and efforts to expand oversight are underway, with many calling for increased accountability and transparency, as seen in the recent discussion on democracynow.org. The need for transparency is not limited to the federal government, with state and local governments also facing challenges in providing information to the public. Ultimately, transparency is essential for a functioning democracy, and efforts to expand oversight and increase accountability are critical for ensuring that government agencies are working in the best interests of the public.

As I reflect on the current state of oversight efforts, I firmly believe that a lack of accountability will ultimately lead to corruption and abuse of power. If nothing changes, it's clear that special interest groups and corrupt officials will be the ones to benefit. My thesis is that robust oversight mechanisms are essential to ensuring transparency and fairness in our systems. Without them, the voices of ordinary citizens will be drowned out, and the powerful will continue to exploit their positions for personal gain.
