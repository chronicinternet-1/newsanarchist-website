US Sanctions Intensify

The US imposes fresh sanctions on entities and individuals linked to Hezbollah's financial network amidst rising tensions with Iran

Elena Vasquez
Former foreign correspondent, 15 years covering conflict zones
July 1, 2026
2 min read
AI-ASSISTED

The US Treasury Department has announced joint sanctions by the Terrorist Financing Targeting Centre (TFTC) against five entities and 16 individuals it claims are linked to Hezbollah’s financial network, as reported by Al Jazeera on the Live Updates: War returns to Iran with Israel, US strikes page. These sanctions are part of a broader effort to intensify pressure on Iran, which has been suffering from crippling economic sanctions and government mismanagement, with the International Monetary Fund (IMF) projecting the country's overall inflation to hit nearly 69 percent in 2026, as noted in a report on Maritime Traffic In Hormuz Strait Slows Sharply. This would be the highest inflation rate since the Islamic Revolution in 1979, and it is likely to have significant implications for the country's economy and political stability.

The US sanctions have been imposed in conjunction with its Gulf partners, and they target individuals and entities allegedly linked to Hezbollah's financial network, according to a report on the US-Iran War Live Updates. The sanctions are part of a larger campaign to disrupt the financial networks of terrorist organizations, and they have been imposed in accordance with the guidelines set out by the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC), which recently issued a general license, as explained on the Last Week's Major Developments in Sanctions page. The US has also imposed sanctions on other companies and individuals, including SBL Energy Ltd and its CEO Alok Choudhari, who are alleged to have supplied explosives to the Sudanese Armed Forces, as reported in an article on US-sanctioned SBL Energy made last Sudan shipment in January.

The intensification of US sanctions is likely to have significant implications for global trade and diplomacy, and it is part of a broader trend of increasing tensions between the US and its adversaries, as discussed in the article US Intensifies Sanctions. The US is also engaged in a series of trade disputes with other countries, including China, which is seeking to China Reshapes Energy markets and expand its influence in the region. Meanwhile, other countries, such as Australia, are taking steps to Australia Blocks China from acquiring strategic assets, in a bid to protect their national security interests.

As I reflect on the escalating US sanctions, I firmly believe that this aggressive approach will only lead to further instability and suffering for innocent civilians.
