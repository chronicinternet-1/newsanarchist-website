# THE TAKE: Follow the $10M AIDS Healthcare Foundation Grant **The real story isn't the money—it's what nobody's asking.** A $10M grant to AIDS Healthcare Foundation just cleared FOIA processing, and mainstream outlets yawned. Standard nonprofit stuff, right? Wrong. Here's what matters: *Who* allocated it? *Which* agency? Under what authority during *which* administration? The FOIA's processed status means documents exist—now demand them. AHF operates clinics across the country. Laudable work. But $10M doesn't materialize in a vacuum. This requires tracing: - Grant application documents - Selection committee composition - Competing proposals rejected - Lobbying records preceding approval The processed FOIA status is your crowbar. The mainstream won't pry. They'll accept the narrative: "Government funds HIV care." Real accountability means naming names on that approval chain and examining *why* this foundation, *this* amount, *this* moment. That's the story.
