IG Oversight Fails

Federal watchdog system for investigating senior inspectors general is missing deadlines and has accountability gaps

Jordan Calloway
Investigative journalist specializing in FOIA litigation
June 24, 2026
4 min read

The federal government's system of oversight for inspectors general has been found to be lacking in accountability, with a recent report from the Government Accountability Office revealing that the Council of the Inspectors General on Integrity and Efficiency, or CIGIE, has failed to meet deadlines and adequately address issues of misconduct among senior inspectors general, as noted in an article on Inspector General Oversight System Fails Accountability. This lack of oversight has led to concerns about the ability of inspectors general to effectively investigate and root out corruption and misconduct within government agencies. For example, a recent story on some stories the felon and his enablers don’t want you to know highlights the need for inspector general study and congressional hearings into the awarding of DHS contracts during the period in which it was overseen by Corey Lewandowski and Steve Miller.

The issue of inadequate oversight is not limited to the inspectors general, as evidenced by the ongoing Covid Transparency Fight, which has seen the government resist efforts to release documents and information related to the pandemic response. Similarly, the Fauci Docs Reveal Biden Admin Intervention story has raised questions about the role of the Biden administration in shaping the narrative around the pandemic. Meanwhile, the DEA Inaction on Fentanyl crisis has highlighted the need for greater transparency and accountability within government agencies. Documents related to these issues can be found on websites such as federalregister.gov, which provides access to federal documents, including those related to the inspectors general and other government agencies.

The lack of oversight and accountability within the inspectors general system has significant implications for the ability of the government to root out corruption and misconduct. As noted in the report, the CIGIE has failed to meet deadlines and adequately address issues of misconduct, which has led to concerns about the effectiveness of the inspectors general in investigating and reporting on corruption and misconduct. The issue is not limited to the inspectors general, but is rather a symptom of a broader problem of lack of transparency and accountability within government agencies. Efforts to address this issue, such as the Covid Transparency Fight, are ongoing, but more needs to be done to ensure that government agencies are transparent and accountable to the public.
