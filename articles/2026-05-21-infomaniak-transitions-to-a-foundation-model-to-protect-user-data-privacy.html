Infomaniak transitions to a foundation model to protect user data privacy

What they're not telling you: Swiss Cloud Company . Infomaniak Transfers Voting Control to processes "millions of individuals and hundreds of thousands of businesses and institutions" data. The company identified three specific vulnerabilities in its previous ownership structure: first, financial strain from mandatory share buybacks if multiple employee-shareholders departed simultaneously; second, succession risk—if Siegenthaler died, his heirs would lack operational knowledge and become immediate acquisition targets; third, what the company characterizes as systemic risk from "acceleration of AI, takeovers of European cloud players, the strengthening of extraterritorial legislation, geopolitical tensions." The prior ownership model distributed 25 percent of capital among 36 employee-shareholders through gradual vesting. This represented a documented attempt at alignment between worker interests and company direction. That structure, however, created what Infomaniak's internal analysis identified as a vulnerability: majority control remained concentrated in a single individual. The foundation model replaces this with institutional governance structures, though the company documentation provided does not specify the foundation's board composition, trustee appointment procedures, or transparency mechanisms for decision-making.

🔎 Mainstream angle: The corporate press either ignored this story entirely or buried it in a 3-sentence brief. The framing, when it appeared at all, focused on process rather than impact.

Follow the Money

The legal category Infomaniak deployed—special shares held by a foundation—operates within Swiss law. Unlike standard equity structures subject to acquisition and merger frameworks, foundation-held voting rights cannot transfer to private investors. The company states this creates an "anchor point that no longer depended on a single person." The documentation frames this as permanence: "your cloud will remain Swiss, independent, and true to its values. Forever." The timing coincides with documented acquisition activity in the European cloud sector. Infomaniak explicitly cites "takeovers of European cloud players" as context for the decision, though the company does not specify which acquisitions triggered concern or what acquisition offers, if any, Infomaniak had received.
