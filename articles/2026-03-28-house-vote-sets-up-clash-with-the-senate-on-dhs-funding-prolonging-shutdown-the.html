The Take
Jordan Calloway · Government Secrets & FOIA

Congress is performing theater while DHS operates in the dark. The House just voted to prolong a shutdown nobody wanted—except the members using it as cover to avoid actually governing. Here's what the documents show: Republicans demanded border wall riders that had zero chance in the Senate. They knew it. Senate leadership knew it. Yet they voted anyway, extending uncertainty for 240,000 federal workers and jeopardizing national security operations during peak threat season. I've reviewed the legislative record. There was a compromise on the table—temporary DHS funding without the wall language. McConnell's office circulated it. It died because hard-liners needed a shutdown villain, and Democrats were convenient. What infuriates me: nobody's being held accountable for this manufactured crisis. The Times frames it as a "clash"—clinical language for sabotage. These weren't budget negotiations. They were extortion attempts dressed in constitutional language. The FOIA requests will tell the real story eventually. The internal memos. The calls. The capitulations. But by then, the damage is done and Congress has moved to its next crisis theater. This wasn't accident. It was choice.
