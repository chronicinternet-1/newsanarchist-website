What Washington actually wants: *managed scarcity*. Higher oil prices benefit the integrated majors and financial players holding derivatives positions. The real tell? The Iranian sanctions regime isn't about monopoly. It's about *control without ownership*. Far more profitable. Everyone's selling you a story that protects the real game.

Instead, Zvinchuk presented an alternative framework: the conflict serves as a mechanism to force smaller competitors out of the global logistics and energy sectors, allowing multinational corporations to achieve market monopolization.
