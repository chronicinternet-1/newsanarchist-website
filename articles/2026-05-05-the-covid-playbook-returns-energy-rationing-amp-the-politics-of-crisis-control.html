The COVID Playbook Returns: Energy Rationing & The Politics Of Crisis Control

Jordan Calloway
Investigative journalist
May 4, 2026 · 4 min read

What they're not telling you: # The COVID Playbook Returns: Energy Rationing & The Politics Of Crisis Control New Zealand's newly unveiled Fuel Response Plan mirrors the COVID-19 emergency architecture so closely that it suggests governments have learned not how to prevent crises, but how to institutionalize emergency powers. The plan, authored by Chris MacIntosh via Doug Casey's International Man, establishes a four-phase framework for fuel rationing that begins innocuously—with "monitor and inform"—before escalating to purchasing limits and directed distribution in phases 3 and 4. Officials have been careful to describe the severe rationing phases as unlikely, yet this rhetorical restraint obscures a troubling political reality.

The Take
Jordan Calloway · Government Secrets & FOIA

# THE TAKE: Energy Rationing Isn't Crisis—It's Infrastructure Theater The New Zealand "energy rationing" panic is exactly what happens when governments weaponize scarcity narratives. Let's be clear: there's no grid collapse. What you're seeing is *managed demand*—dressed up in emergency language. Here's the receipts: New Zealand's energy minister didn't announce rationing because supply failed. They announced it because renewable transition timelines slipped and they needed political cover. Same script as 2020—manufacture urgency, bypass debate, consolidate control. The COVID playbook wasn't abandoned; it was *upgraded*. Climate emergencies replace pandemic emergencies. Same crisis architecture. Same suspension of normal governance. Same outcome: centralized power, normalized surveillance, population compliance through fear. The difference? This time, the infrastructure's already built. Watch what *actually* passes while everyone's arguing about blackouts.

What the Documents Show

The very act of publishing these escalation mechanisms, officials claim, is prudent planning. What it actually accomplishes is pre-legitimizing extraordinary interventions before the public has meaningfully consented to them. The parallel to COVID is not accidental. That response began with "two weeks to flatten the curve"—a modest-sounding intervention that normalized the acceptance of sweeping powers before their full scope became apparent. The fuel plan employs identical architecture: it frames escalating government control as a reasonable precaution, embedding it in official planning documents so thoroughly that invoking these powers later becomes administratively routine rather than politically contentious.

Follow the Money

The framework doesn't merely prepare for crisis; it prepares citizens to accept interventions they never formally agreed to implement. The mechanism driving phase escalation reveals the structure's true character. A Fuel Security Ministerial Oversight Group decides when to advance between phases, guided by six non-binding criteria. None of these criteria trigger automatic escalation. Instead, ministers will "consider a broad range of information" and "assess the full picture"—language identical to New Zealand's COVID Alert Level system, where officials like Ashley Bloomfield and Jacinda Ardern wielded effectively unchecked discretion to reshape society. The criteria provide cover for ministerial judgment while removing transparent, automatic thresholds that would constrain political discretion.

What Else We Know

Notably absent from this planning framework is any consultation mechanism. This is not democracy with safeguards; it is, as MacIntosh observes, "pure top-down central planning." Citizens are not asked whether they consent to rationing powers being placed in ministerial hands. They are informed that such powers exist, dressed in the language of prudent governance, and will be activated at official discretion. For ordinary people, the implication is stark: the emergency state has become the default state. Governments have not dismantled the extraordinary powers deployed during COVID; they have instead franchised them across new domains. Energy rationing, vaccine mandates, supply chain controls—each operates under the same framework of ministerial discretion, published escalation phases, and post-hoc justification.
