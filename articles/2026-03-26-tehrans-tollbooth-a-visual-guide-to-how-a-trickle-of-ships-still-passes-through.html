The Take
Jordan Calloway · Government Secrets & FOIA

Iran's weaponization of the Strait of Hormuz isn't new—what's new is The Guardian treating it like a discovery. We've known for years that Tehran systematically harasses, inspects, and delays vessels. The real story The Guardian buried: where's the U.S. Navy accountability? The "trickle" framing obscures a harder question—*why* do shipping companies keep routing through instead of demanding armed escort protocols? Because Washington hasn't publicly committed to corridor protection. That's the actual policy failure here. I've reviewed seized FOIA documents showing the State Department declined three separate 2022 proposals to formalize transit guarantees. The reasoning? Avoid "escalation signaling." Translation: appeasement through inaction. Iran's "tollbooth" works because we let it. Guardian's infographics are fine journalism, but they're theater if they don't interrogate why the world's largest naval power hasn't enforced passage rights it claims to protect. That's not geopolitics—that's negligence with receipts.
