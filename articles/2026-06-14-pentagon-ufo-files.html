Pentagon releases third batch of declassified UFO files

The Pentagon has released a third batch of declassified UFO files, shedding more light on the mysterious sightings that have been investigated by the government over the years. According to reports, the latest batch of files includes sightings from around the world, and reveals that the government has been taking a closer look at these phenomena. The release of these files is a significant development, and comes after years of speculation and rumors about the government's knowledge of UFOs.

One of the key figures involved in the release of these files is Kirkpatrick, who has been briefing Senate committees on the sightings. In a public meeting of NASA's UAP independent study team on May 31, 2023, Kirkpatrick shared an infrared video recorded in the western United States in 2021, which was analyzed by the Pentagon's All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO). The analysis, combined with commercial flight data, led the AARO to conclude that the sightings were likely commercial aircraft. However, another video released by the Pentagon on April 19, 2023, featuring MQ-9 drone footage from the Middle East, shows an unidentified aerial phenomenon that resembles a small "metallic orb".

The latest batch of files includes some vivid descriptions of mysterious sightings, including a report and digital recreation of a UFO spotted near Cheyenne Mountain, a critical national security site. The UAP was described as being shaped like a potato, with fish-like scales and a white opalescent color. Other files describe spinning discs and glowing orbs, which have been reported by multiple witnesses. While the government has not provided any conclusive evidence of extraterrestrial life, the release of these files suggests that they are taking a more serious look at these phenomena.

The release of these files has also sparked controversy, with some critics accusing the government of covering up the truth about UFOs. According to reports, representatives from the AARO have been contacting whistleblowers and attempting to silence them. One whistleblower, who testified before a Senate committee, reported being contacted by AARO representatives and eventually having his chain of command reached out to. The government has not commented on these allegations, but the release of these files suggests that they are trying to be more transparent about their investigations into UFOs.

The cost of investigating UFOs is not insignificant, with the government allocating millions of dollars to the AARO each year. While the exact amount is not publicly disclosed, it is reported to be in the tens of millions of dollars.
