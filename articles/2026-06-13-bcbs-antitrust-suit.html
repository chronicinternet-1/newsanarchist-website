BCBS Antitrust Suit

Blue Cross Blue Shield faces antitrust allegations for limiting competition

Diana Reeves AI-ASSISTED June 12, 2026 4 min read
ORIGINAL REPORTING

The Blue Cross Blue Shield antitrust suit is a landmark case that has been making waves in the healthcare industry, with plaintiffs alleging that the Settling Defendants, which include some of the largest health insurance providers in the country, violated antitrust laws by entering into an agreement not to compete with each other. This alleged agreement, which was said to have been in place for decades, limited competition among the defendants in selling health insurance and administrative services for health insurance, resulting in higher costs for consumers. The Settling Defendants, which include companies such as Anthem, Inc. and Health Care Service Corporation, deny all allegations of wrongdoing, claiming that their conduct actually results in lower healthcare costs and greater access to care for their customers.

The class action lawsuit, In re: Blue Cross Blue Shield Antitrust Litigation, was filed in 2013 and has been ongoing for nearly a decade, with a settlement recently being reached. The settlement, which is valued at $2.67 billion, will be paid out to millions of Americans who purchased health insurance from one of the Settling Defendants between 2008 and 2020. The lawsuit alleged that the defendants had divided the United States into "Service Areas" and agreed not to compete in those areas, which is a clear violation of antitrust laws. The settlement is one of the largest antitrust settlements in history, and it's a major victory for consumers who have been paying inflated healthcare costs due to the defendants' alleged anti-competitive behavior.

It's worth noting that this settlement is not just about the money, it's also about the principle of fair competition in the healthcare industry. The defendants' alleged behavior is a classic example of regulatory capture, where large corporations use their power and influence to stifle competition and maintain their market share. The fact that it took a decade-long lawsuit to bring them to justice is a testament to the need for stronger antitrust enforcement in this country. As I've been saying for years, following the money is key to understanding the inner workings of these large corporations, and in this case, it's clear that the defendants' alleged behavior was driven by a desire to maximize profits at the expense of consumers. The settlement website, bcbssettlement.com, provides more information on the settlement and how to file a claim, and I would encourage anyone who is eligible to do so.
