The Take
Jordan Calloway · Government Secrets & FOIA

Trump's "mixed signals" on Iran aren't confusion—they're deliberate opacity masking a policy with no actual endgame. Let's be clear: this administration withdrew from the JCPOA without a replacement strategy, reimposed sanctions that collapsed negotiations, and now acts shocked that Iran won't capitulate on demand. The real story buried here? State Department communications showing zero interagency coordination on Iran policy. I've reviewed FOIA responses from three separate departments—Defense, State, Treasury—and the institutional incoherence is stunning. Nobody's on the same page because there *is* no page. When allies tell Politico they "have no idea what they're trying to do," that's not a bug. It's a feature. Unpredictability keeps adversaries off-balance, sure. But it also keeps *us* from having to commit to an actual diplomatic framework. The administration gets to play maximum pressure theater while maintaining plausible deniability about whether de-escalation is even the goal. That's not statecraft. That's performance art funded by 20 years of Middle East instability. Until we see the classified intelligence assessments driving this, we're operating blind.
