    For those keeping count...it's still zero.

    Rule 10 Submission Statement: The United States has made exactly two major arrests directly connected to the Jeffrey Epstein sex-trafficking ring: Epstein himself and his primary accomplice, Ghislaine Maxwell.     What they're not telling you: For Those Keeping Count…It's Still Zero The United States federal government has secured exactly two major convictions directly connected to the Jeffrey Epstein sex-trafficking enterprise since 2008: Jeffrey Epstein himself and Ghislaine Maxwell. That is the entire institutional accounting. This fact becomes starker when placed against what followed.

    What the Documents Show

    After Epstein's arrest in July 2019 and Maxwell's conviction in December 2021, the public was assured by federal prosecutors that investigations would expand—that the case files would yield additional prosecutions. Southern District of New York prosecutors, who handled the Maxwell case, spoke confidently about the scope of evidence. Instead, what the country received was a partial release of court documents in January 2024, followed by silence. The timeline matters. Epstein died in Manhattan federal custody on August 10, 2019, while awaiting trial.

    🔎 Mainstream angle
    The corporate press either ignored this story entirely or buried it in a 3-sentence brief. The framing, when it appeared at all, focused on process rather than impact.

    Follow the Money

    The medical examiner ruled it a suicide. That same year, Maxwell fled, was captured in New Hampshire in July 2020, and faced trial before Judge Alison J. District Court for the Southern District of New York. Prosecutors presented evidence of decades of trafficking, naming dozens of unnamed victims and several named conspirators. The court record included testimony implicating people in government, finance, and media by profession if not always by name. Maxwell received an 80-year sentence in June 2022.

    What Else We Know

    Since then: zero additional federal prosecutions of co-conspirators. No arrests of the named individuals whose names appeared in flight logs, police records, or victim testimony. No indictments of lawyers, accountants, security personnel, or household staff who maintained the operation. No charges against financial intermediaries who moved money through the networks Epstein used. What the official account claims is straightforward—that this was primarily a two-person conspiracy, that investigative pathways led nowhere prosecutable, that the system worked because two criminals faced justice. The evidence from the trial record contradicts this.
