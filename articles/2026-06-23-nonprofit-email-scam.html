Nonprofit Email Scam

Scammers used a nonprofit email account to charge Fresno County $1.5M in fake charges, according to the DOJ

Jordan Ames
Former federal fraud investigator, 9 years tracking benefits fraud
June 23, 2026
4 min read
AI-ASSISTED

A recent indictment has shed light on a nonprofit email scam that resulted in $1.5 million in fake charges to Fresno County, as reported by the Fresno Bee. The scheme involved scammers gaining control of an email account used by the finance department of a nonprofit organization, allowing them to secretly authorize fraudulent transactions. Nyangoro and Peter Bah Acha, 45, of Berlin, Germany, were named in the six-count indictment, which included conspiracy to commit wire fraud and wire fraud related to the scheme.

The case highlights the growing concern of wire fraud and money laundering in nonprofit organizations, as seen in the recent indictment of the Southern Poverty Law Center, which allegedly funneled over $3 million in donor money, according to a report on rushbabe49.com. This incident has sparked controversy, with some critics questioning the actions of Senator Jon Ossoff of Georgia, who received over $700,000 from the SPLC. The indictment of the SPLC has also raised concerns about the lack of transparency and accountability in nonprofit organizations.

In a similar case, a notorious hacker was convicted of 14 counts of felony wire fraud, as reported on thedrive.com. The hacker's actions involved social engineering tactics, including gaining access to sensitive login data. This case highlights the importance of cybersecurity measures in preventing wire fraud and other forms of financial crime. The crackdown on fraud has been gaining momentum, with a recent judge blocking SNAP restrictions and a Medicare fraud mastermind caught, resulting in a significant fraud crackdown worth $14.6 billion.

The nonprofit email scam has raised concerns about the vulnerability of nonprofit organizations to financial crime, and the need for increased transparency and accountability in these organizations. The case is ongoing, with further investigations and indictments expected in the coming months. Stay up to date on the latest developments in this case and other indictments on AP News.

The Jordan Ames Take

As someone who has worked with nonprofits, I am appalled by the rise of nonprofit email scams. In my opinion, these scams are a threat to the very fabric of our charitable sector. If nothing changes, the scammers will continue to win, deceiving unsuspecting donors and diverting much-needed funds away from legitimate causes. I firmly believe that it is our responsibility to take action against these scams, and I propose that we implement stricter email verification processes and educate the public on how to spot fake charity emails. If we fail to act, the scammers will be the only ones who win.
