A Reckoning Is Underway At The FDA

Jordan Calloway
Investigative journalist
April 21, 2026 · 4 min read

What they're not telling you: Authored by Maryanne Demasi via The Brownstone Institute, For months, a quiet battle has been unfolding inside the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA). It began with an analysis of child deaths after Covid vaccination, followed by strategic leaks to major media outlets, and has now erupted into the open with a memo from the regulator’s own vaccine chief. In September, it was reported that FDA officials had privately investigated 25 paediatric deaths following Covid vaccination — the first systematic review of such cases since the rollout began.

The Take
Casey North · Unexplained & Emerging Tech

# The FDA's "Reckoning" Is Really Just Kabuki Let's cut through the theater: the FDA isn't reckoning with anything—it's performing contrition while maintaining the same gatekeeping machinery. Yes, internal dissent is brewing. Yes, some scientists are pushing back against bureaucratic inertia. But a "quiet battle" doesn't equal systemic change. The agency will shuffle a few names, issue apologetic memos, maybe accelerate one approval pathway. Then business resumes. The real issue? The FDA simultaneously protects public health AND protects pharmaceutical profits. These aren't compatible mandates, yet we pretend they coexist. Until we confront that structural conflict—not celebrate leaked complaints—nothing fundamental shifts. A genuine reckoning would mean dismantling approval theater and accepting uncertainty. The FDA will never choose that. Neither will Congress. Watch what happens after the headlines fade.

What the Documents Show

The findings were meant to be presented to the CDC’s Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP). But the presentation never came. The meeting passed without a word. Something had happened behind closed doors. On 13 November 2025, STAT published an extraordinary insider account describing a tense internal meeting in which FDA scientist Dr Tracy Beth Høeg presented evidence of young people who had died after Covid vaccination.

🔎 Mainstream angle: The corporate press either ignored this story entirely or buried it in a 3-sentence brief. The framing, when it appeared at all, focused on process rather than impact.

Follow the Money

According to STAT, her findings triggered pushback from career FDA regulators who feared the implications of acknowledging fatal cases. Now, comes the explosive memo from FDA vaccine chief Dr Vinay Prasad, confirming — for the first time — that US regulators have formally attributed at least 10 of these children’s deaths to Covid vaccination. Prasad called it “a profound revelation” with far-reaching implications for American vaccine policy, adding that the true number is “certainly an underestimate.” Here, I’ll take you through the memo, the leaks, the internal rebellion at FDA, and what this means — not just for Covid vaccines, but for all vaccine approvals going forward. This story marks a turning point in US vaccine regulation. In early September, insiders at the FDA and CDC quietly told the New York Times and the Washington Post that the agency had begun investigating child deaths reported to VAERS. My reporting confirmed that Dr Tracy Beth Høeg, a senior adviser within the FDA’s vaccine division, had led the review — contacting families, gathering medical records, and obtaining autopsy findings.

What Else We Know

It was the first case-by-case evaluation of paediatric deaths conducted since the vaccines were authorised. The review identified twenty-five children whose deaths occurred following vaccination. Those findings were expected to be presented to ACIP on 18–19 September. Instead, without explanation, the discussion disappeared from the agenda. Even FDA Commissioner Dr Marty Makary had hinted at the findings on CNN, saying, “We’ve been looking into the VAERS database self-reports, [and] there have been children that have died from the Covid vaccine.” He described an “intense” investigation involving doctors, autopsies, and family interviews. Yet ACIP heard nothing.
