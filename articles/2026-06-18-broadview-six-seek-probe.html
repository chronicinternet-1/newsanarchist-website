Broadview Six Seek Probe

Now-cleared protesters request special prosecutor over alleged prosecutorial misconduct

The Broadview Six, a group of protesters who were charged with conspiracy over a protest at the Broadview detention facility in Illinois, are seeking a special prosecutor to investigate government lawyers involved in their case. The request comes after a federal judge, April Perry, discovered prosecutorial misconduct in transcripts from grand jury proceedings. The case against the Broadview Six imploded in dramatic fashion last month, with the charges being dropped amidst revelations of serious misconduct.

The protesters are asking Judge Perry to appoint a special prosecutor to investigate the government lawyers involved in the case, including those from the office of John Lausch, the US Attorney for the Northern District of Illinois. The Broadview Six claim that the government's actions constitute criminal contempt, and that a special prosecutor is needed to investigate the matter. The request is not unprecedented, as there have been similar cases in the past where special prosecutors have been appointed to investigate government misconduct.

In a similar case in Minnesota, 15 people were charged with conspiracy over anti-ICE protests, with three activists being convicted of conspiracy charges in Spokane, Washington. The federal government has been cracking down on immigration protests, with the Broadview Six case being one of the most high-profile examples. The case has drawn attention to the issue of prosecutorial misconduct, with many arguing that the government's actions were intended to intimidate and silence protesters.

The Broadview Six are being represented by a team of attorneys who are pushing for a full accounting of the government's actions in the case. The attorneys have filed a series of motions and briefs, arguing that the government's misconduct was not limited to the prosecution of the Broadview Six, but was part of a larger pattern of behavior. The case is being closely watched by civil liberties groups and advocates for immigrants' rights, who argue that the government's actions are an attack on the right to free speech and assembly.

Judge Perry has yet to rule on the request for a special prosecutor, but the Broadview Six remain optimistic that their request will be granted.
