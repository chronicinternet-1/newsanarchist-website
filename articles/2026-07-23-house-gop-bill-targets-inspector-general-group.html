House GOP Bill Targets Inspector General Group

A new House GOP bill proposes to abolish the Council of the Inspectors General on Integrity and Efficiency

Jordan Calloway

July 23, 2026
1 min read
AI-ASSISTED

A new House GOP bill, the Restoring Integrity and Efficiency to Inspector General Oversight Act of 2025, introduced by Representative Clay Higgins, would abolish the Council of the Inspectors General on Integrity and Efficiency (CIGIE) one year after enactment, as reported by the House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform. The move comes after a recent Government Accountability Office report found that CIGIE frequently missed requirements when investigating misconduct allegations against inspectors general, according to Government Executive. This development is part of a broader effort to improve government operations and prevent fraud, as inspectors general are trying to prioritize preventing fraud, rather than responding to it, similar to efforts to address corporate fraud cases and ensure electronic delivery rule compliance, as proposed by the SEC. The Office of Management and Budget would take over establishing IG employee training standards, supervising interagency investigations, and resolving jurisdictional disputes under the new bill. Additionally, the Government Accountability Office would develop IG inspection standards, and Federal Law Enforcement Training Centers would maintain at least one training academy for IG employees. A Republican Oversight Committee aide stated that "continued failures and abuses by [CIGIE's] Integrity Committee have shown the inspectors general cannot be trusted to investigate wrongdoing in their ranks," citing the need to end the Integrity Committee and transfer CIGIE's core functions to restore trust in the IG system. The Project on Government Oversight nonprofit argued that transferring many of CIGIE's responsibilities to OMB represents "a profound structural and philosophical change" that could create an unavoidable conflict of interest and undermine inspector general independence. Meanwhile, local inspectors general, such as the one in Jacksonville, continue to uncover issues like unpermitted work in school kitchens, as reported by news4jax.com. CIGIE is currently being led by Cheryl Mason, the IG for the Veterans Affairs Department, whose ability to provide independent oversight has been questioned by Democrats and good government groups due to her previous role in the Trump administration.
