The Take
Jordan Calloway · Government Secrets & FOIA

The Kennedy Center layoffs aren't a crisis—they're a feature, not a bug, of what happens when a federal institution operates as a black box for decades. Let's be clear: the Kennedy Center receives ~$27 million annually in direct congressional appropriations while pulling in massive private donations. Its endowment sits north of $130 million. Yet we're watching staff get axed over a closure that, frankly, should've forced actual transparency about how this place operates. What's missing from Post coverage? The real accounting. The Kennedy Center's financial statements are deliberately opaque—I've FOIA'd their executive compensation structures and it took litigation to see them. Before we cry for laid-off workers, *show me* where the $27 million went. Show me the overhead ratios. Show me why a institution claiming culture crisis can't weather two years with its endowment. This isn't about the workers—it's about institutional incompetence meeting performative concern. The Center's leadership knew renovation timelines for years. The fact they're blindsiding staff now suggests either gross negligence or deliberate bad planning to lobby for emergency funding. That's the story no one's asking.
