What they're not telling you: The Privacy Trap: Why . Free Apps Cost You Everything. The average American has surrendered their digital exhaust—email metadata, keystroke patterns, authentication logs, and password vault contents—to five corporations, and no regulator has ever calculated what that surrender is worth. A Reddit user's simple request for privacy-friendly alternatives to Gmail, Google Keyboard, Google Keep, Google Authenticator, and Chrome's password manager exposes a market structure so thoroughly colonized by surveillance capitalism that "free" has become an accounting fiction. The user wants five things: email, keyboard input, note-taking, two-factor authentication, and password management.

What the Documents Show

Each category represents a discrete data stream. Each stream has quantifiable value to Google, Microsoft, and Apple. And each transaction occurs in regulatory darkness. Here's the architecture. When you type on Google Keyboard, every keystroke—including passwords you later delete, search queries you abandon, intimate confessions in messaging apps—flows to Google's servers.

🔎 Mainstream angle
The corporate press either ignored this story entirely or buried it in a 3-sentence brief. The framing, when it appeared at all, focused on process rather than impact.

Follow the Money

Google doesn't charge you. Instead, it monetizes behavioral prediction. Alphabet reported $307.4 billion in revenue in 2023, with advertising comprising $237.9 billion of that total. The keyboard is a data collection instrument disguised as a utility. The user pays zero dollars. Alphabet's ad customers pay millions for the inference models built from aggregated keystroke data.

What Else We Know

The 2FA and password manager categories reveal the same pattern. When you store 200 passwords in Google's system, you're not purchasing security. You're licensing your authentication surface to an advertising company. That surface contains behavioral signatures—the sites you visit, the services you use, your financial institutions, your medical providers. Google can infer your health status from your password manager. It can model your political orientation.
