NDEs as Brain Dreams

Researcher Kayış proposes the dying-moment dream hypothesis to explain near-death experiences

Researchers have long been fascinated by the phenomenon of near-death experiences, and a new theory proposes that these events may be the result of the brain generating a dream state in response to oxygen deprivation, as outlined in a recent article on New Theory Explains Why So Many People See the Same Thing During a Near-Death Experience. This concept, known as the dying-moment dream hypothesis, was put forth by researcher Kayış, who suggests that the brain's attempt to make sense of the impending death can result in the characteristic tunnel vision, feelings of peace, and sense of detachment that are commonly reported by those who have had near-death experiences.

The idea that near-death experiences may be the brain's final dream-like simulation has been gaining traction, with a recent paper published in Frontiers in Psychology proposing this very theory, as reported on New Theory Suggests Near-Death Experiences May Be the Brain's Final Dream Before Death. While this concept is still purely speculative, it has sparked a lively debate among researchers and experts in the field, including Dr. Roger Nelson, whose work can be found in articles such as Dr. Roger Nelson. The Vera Solano Take
Metaphysical & Consciousness

As I reflect on the concept of Near Death Experiences, I firmly believe that NDEs can be explained as brain dreams. Our brains are wired to create experiences, even in the absence of external stimuli. If we continue to attribute NDEs to paranormal or spiritual forces without considering the scientific explanation, skeptics and debunkers win. They will continue to dismiss the experiences of those who have had NDEs, leaving them without a legitimate outlet for discussion. I propose that we approach NDEs with a more nuanced understanding, acknowledging the complex workings of the human brain and its ability to create vivid, dream-like experiences, even in the most extreme circumstances.
