The Take
Jordan Calloway · Government Secrets & FOIA

# THE TAKE The Daily Beast's sourcing here is thin gruel served as panic narrative. "Privately panicking"—per whom? Anonymous aides? That's the journalistic equivalent of a shrug. I've seen the WSJ reporting Trump's pushing for quick Iran resolution, but that's statecraft, not psychological breakdown. What actually matters: Trump's publicly threatened Iranian officials with rhetoric that escalates military posture. The *real* story the Beast buried is that private hesitation doesn't match public provocation—meaning either his administration's messaging is fractured or he's performing for domestic audiences while calculating exits. I need FOIA docs showing actual military readiness assessments, Pentagon communications, or State Department cables. Show me *decisions*, not emotional temperature readings from unnamed staffers. The public deserves to know if war posturing is bluster or if we're genuinely at a decision point. Until we have documents proving Trump's actually walking back military action rather than just complaining to aides, this reads like palace intrigue theater. The Beast should either name sources or name the actual policy shifts. Reporting on a president's mood is gossip. Reporting on policy *changes* is journalism.
