EU Targets Russia Drone Supply

EU plans new sanctions on companies supporting Russia's military-industrial complex

The European Union is planning to impose fresh sanctions targeting Russia's drone supply chain, according to EU foreign policy chief Kaja Kallas, who announced the proposal in response to the latest wave of Russian attacks on Ukraine, as reported by the Kyiv Post. This move is part of a broader effort to weaken Russia's military capabilities and comes on the heels of recent actions taken by the US, including the re-imposition of sanctions on Iranian oil sales, which has sparked a new wave of tensions in the region, with some analysts suggesting that the war speeds energy shift towards alternative sources.

The US strikes on Iran and the subsequent re-imposition of sanctions have been widely condemned, with Iranian officials announcing that the US had violated the June 18 agreement intended to ease tensions, as reported by the New York Times. The situation has been further complicated by the involvement of other global powers, including China, which has been accused of coercion by the US, with officials warning of the dangers of US warns of China coercion in the region.

The EU's decision to target Russia's drone supply chain is seen as a significant escalation of tensions between the two sides, with some analysts suggesting that it could have far-reaching consequences for the global economy. The US has also taken recent actions to impose sanctions on Russian companies, as outlined on the ofac.treasury.gov website, which provides details of recent actions taken by the Office of Foreign Assets Control.
