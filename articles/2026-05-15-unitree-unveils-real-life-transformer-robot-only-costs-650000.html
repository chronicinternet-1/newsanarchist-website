What the Documents Show

The bipedal mecha transforms into a giant robo-dog configuration. But the dual-use potential is explicit in Unitree's own framing: the company positions its robotics line—including the A2 and B2 quadruped units already deployed in "inspection scenarios"—as tools for "high-risk and harsh environments." Unitree's rapid verticalization of robotic technology mirrors China's broader industrial strategy: move faster than regulatory frameworks can respond. The company has already deployed quadruped robots into operational settings under the consumer and inspection banner.
