    Feds Subpoena Hasan Piker, CodePink Cofounder Over "Humanitarian" Trip To Communist Cuba

    Marcus Webb
    Former intelligence contractor
    May 24, 2026
    4 min read

    What they're not telling you: THE STORY The Treasury Department's Office of Foreign Assets Control has issued formal subpoenas to content creator Hasan Piker and CodePink cofounder Medea Benjamin demanding records of their December 2023 travel to Cuba, marking the first documented enforcement action targeting participants in the "Nuestra América Convoy" delegation. According to reporting from Fox News citing Treasury Department sources, OFAC issued "Requests for Information" subpoenas examining whether Piker and Benjamin violated the Cuban Assets Control Regulations—the statutory framework governing U.S. sanctions enforcement against Cuba.

    What the Documents Show

    The subpoenas specifically target financial records, communications, and documentation of goods transferred during the trip. OFAC's mandate under 31 U.S.C. § 5321 requires investigators to determine whether prohibited transactions occurred: whether the subjects financed humanitarian deliveries, coordinated supply movements, or engaged in financial transfers that circumvented the decades-long embargo framework. The investigation focuses on three measurable elements. First, financial flows: investigators are examining whether Piker and Benjamin funded or financed goods deliveries to Cuba directly or through intermediaries.

    🔎 Mainstream angle
    The corporate press either ignored this story entirely or buried it in a 3-sentence brief. The framing, when it appeared at all, focused on process rather than impact.

    Follow the Money

    Second, coordination: OFAC is seeking evidence of whether the delegation coordinated logistics with Cuban government entities or regime-aligned organizations—specifically the Cuban Institute for Friendship with the Peoples (ICAP), a state apparatus documented by the U.S. State Department as operating to "develop foreign activist networks for overseas statecraft operations." Third, intelligence liaison: the investigation is examining whether participants met with personnel from the Dirección General de Inteligencia (DGI), Cuba's foreign intelligence service. The "Nuestra América Convoy" operated as a documented network spanning multiple continents. Participants included Piker, Benjamin, and—according to available reporting—Isra Hirsi, daughter of U.S. Representative Ilhan Omar. The convoy transported supplies described by Benjamin in public statements as "thousands of pounds of goods" to Cuba.

    What Else We Know

    Piker documented aspects of the delegation on his Twitch streaming platform, where he has an audience exceeding 700,000 followers. The subpoenas request all communications, travel itineraries, receipts, banking records, and documentation of goods transferred. What distinguishes this enforcement action from routine travel audits is the scope: OFAC rarely initiates document requests against U.S. citizens engaged in humanitarian activism without preliminary evidence of sanctions violations. The fact that subpoenas were issued to both Piker and Benjamin—rather than civil penalty notices—indicates prosecutors believe the evidence warrants potential violations of 50 U.S.C. § 1705, which carries criminal penalties.
