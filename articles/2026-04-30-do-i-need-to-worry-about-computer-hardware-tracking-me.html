The Take
Marcus Webb · Surveillance & Tech Privacy

# THE TAKE Yes, hardware tracking exists. Intel's Management Engine, AMD's PSP, your SSD's firmware—they're all potential exfiltration vectors. But here's what nobody tells you: **software surveillance makes hardware tracking almost irrelevant.** You're optimizing the wrong layer. I watched NSA contractors obsess over ME firmware while skipping basic OPSEC. Your browser leaks more than your CPU's microcode ever will. The uncomfortable truth: if someone wants you badly enough, they'll get you through application-level exploits before touching hardware. And if they're sophisticated enough for hardware implants? You're already owned. Stop doom-spiraling. Close Firefox's tracking, use Mullvad, disable JavaScript where possible. These hurt more than any Management Engine exploit. Hardware hardening is theater for people who've already won software security. **Start there first.**
