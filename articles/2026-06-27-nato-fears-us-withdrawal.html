NATO Fears US Withdrawal

NATO leaders fear they can no longer rely on US help if Russia attacks Eastern Europe

Elena Vasquez
Former foreign correspondent, 15 years covering conflict zones
June 27, 2026
4 min read
AI-ASSISTED

NATO leaders are growing increasingly concerned about the potential withdrawal of US support in the face of a Russian attack, as reported by Nato leaders fear they can no longer rely on US help if Russia attacks. This fear is not unfounded, given the recent actions of the US administration, which has been unpredictable in its foreign policy decisions. The North Atlantic Treaty Organization is a defensive alliance of 32 countries from Europe and North America, with a mission to defend its member countries and their one billion citizens.

The conflict in Ukraine has further exacerbated tensions between NATO and Russia, with the UK and NATO ruling out the establishment of a no-fly zone in Ukraine, as discussed in the House of Commons Library. Planning for the post-conflict reconstruction of Ukraine has begun, with the use of sanctioned Russian assets to help fund reconstruction being considered. Meanwhile, President Volodymyr Zelensky has signed decrees implementing a decision by the National Security and Defense Council of Ukraine to extend sanctions against entities supporting Russia's military-industrial complex.

In related news, the US has imposed sanctions on eight individuals over the Sudan war, as detailed in the article US Sanctions 8 Over Sudan War. This move is seen as a significant step in addressing the ongoing conflict in the region. Additionally, China has surpassed a global record, prompting concerns about its growing influence, as reported in China Surpasses Global Record. The US-China ties have also been strained, with tensions rising over various issues, including trade and security, as discussed in US-China Ties Strained. The House of Representatives Committee on Rules has also announced the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2027, which will be considered on June 29, 2026.

The potential withdrawal of US support from NATO has significant implications for global security, particularly in the face of an increasingly aggressive Russia. The deployment of troops to the Baltics under the NATO umbrella, including a full brigade of German troops to Lithuania, is a symbol of Europe's seriousness in addressing the situation. However, the lack of a clear commitment from the US has raised concerns about the ability of NATO to defend its member countries effectively. As the situation continues to unfold, it remains to be seen how the US will respond to the growing fears of NATO leaders.

The Elena Vasquez Take
Global Power & Geopolitics

As I reflect on the current state of global politics, I strongly believe that a US withdrawal from NATO would be a catastrophic mistake. In my opinion, if the US were to withdraw from the alliance, Russia would be the ultimate winner. Without American support, NATO's ability to deter Russian aggression would be severely weakened, allowing Putin to expand his influence across Eastern Europe. This would not only undermine regional stability but also embolden other authoritarian regimes around the world. I firmly believe that it is essential for the US to maintain its commitment to NATO to prevent Russia from filling the power vacuum.
