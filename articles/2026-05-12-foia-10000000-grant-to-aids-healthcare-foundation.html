What they're not telling you: # FOIA Request Reveals $10 Million HUD Grant to AIDS Healthcare Foundation Under Scrutiny A Freedom of Information Act request filed with the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development has exposed a $10 million federal grant to AIDS Healthcare Foundation that apparently triggered internal HUD reconsideration due to the organization's conduct as a landlord. What the Documents Show

The requester explicitly asked for records showing whether HUD reconsidered the award based on "AHF's actions as a landlord"—language suggesting the agency had concerns about how the organization manages properties beyond its healthcare operations. This distinction matters. While AIDS Healthcare Foundation is widely known for providing HIV treatment and related services, its parallel role as a significant real estate holder has drawn less mainstream attention. The FOIA filing indicates HUD officials may have grappled with whether landlord conduct should factor into federal healthcare funding decisions—a question that cuts to how government evaluates organizational fitness for public money. The request also demands records of all communications "whether the communication occurred before or after the grant of funds," signaling that the requester suspects ongoing HUD deliberation about the grant even after funds were disbursed.
