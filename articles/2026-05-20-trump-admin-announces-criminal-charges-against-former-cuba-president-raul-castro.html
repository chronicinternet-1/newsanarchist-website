Trump Admin Announces Criminal Charges Against Former Cuba President Raul Castro

Jordan Calloway
Investigative journalist
May 20, 2026 · 3 min read

What they're not telling you: # TRUMP ADMIN RESURRECTS 1996 CUBA INCIDENT AS POLITICAL WEAPON AGAINST 94-YEAR-OLD CASTRO The Trump administration unsealed a 30-year-old indictment against former Cuban President Raúl Castro on Wednesday, charging the 94-year-old with conspiracy to murder U.S. nationals and destruction of aircraft—crimes that occurred in 1996 when Cuban MiG fighter jets shot down two civilian planes operated by Brothers to the Rescue, a Miami-based exile group. Here's what the official narrative obscures: The Justice Department did not spontaneously decide this week to prosecute a charge it had shelved for three decades.

What the Documents Show

The timing is pure political theater. According to the source material, Republican members of Congress held a press conference *that same Wednesday morning* condemning Castro before the indictment was unsealed *later that day*. This is not investigation driving disclosure. This is disclosure following political choreography. Carlos Gimenez, R-Fla., and other congressional Republicans had pressured the DOJ for years to bring charges, but the Trump administration's move to unseal the indictment—coordinated with a morning press conference—reveals the decision was made to maximize political leverage, not justice.

🔎 Mainstream angle: The corporate press either ignored this story entirely or buried it in a 3-sentence brief. The framing, when it appeared at all, focused on process rather than impact.

Follow the Money

The incident itself is genuine. In 1996, Cuban jets fired on two unarmed civilian aircraft outside Cuban airspace, killing all four men aboard. The legal questions are straightforward: jurisdiction, statute of limitations, and whether a sitting president can be prosecuted for actions taken in his official capacity 28 years earlier. None of these questions are addressed in the unsealing. What's absent from the framing is equally important. The source material reveals that Cuban exiles and their congressional allies have been pressuring the DOJ specifically to indict not just Raúl Castro but also his late brother Fidel and others.

What Else We Know

This is not a neutral prosecutorial decision responding to new evidence. This is political prosecution following 30 years of lobbying by a faction with a direct interest in destabilizing the Cuban regime. The Trump administration is weaponizing the criminal justice system to satisfy a constituency that never accepted the 1996 incident as closed. The unsealing also reveals something darker about contemporary prosecutorial discretion: the DOJ can hold indictments in reserve for political moments. If an indictment existed for 30 years without unsealing, the question becomes why unseal it now? The answer appears in the source material itself—the Trump administration is attempting to "force change on the island after nearly seven decades of communist rule." This is not criminal justice.
