Sudan Crisis Escalates

US sanctions Indian firm for fuelling Sudan's civil war, worsening humanitarian crisis

Rafael Reyes
War correspondent, 11 years covering unreported conflicts
June 28, 2026
4 min read
AI-ASSISTED

The Sudan crisis has escalated significantly, with the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) militants closing in on the strategic city of El Obeid, prompting experts and rights groups to warn of a "genocide-like" event, similar to what unfolded in El Fasher, as reported by ndtv.com. The situation has drawn international attention, with the United Nations warning that the window to prevent a major escalation in El Obeid is "rapidly narrowing" as fighting intensifies in and around the North Kordofan state capital, according to a report by the UN News. The conflict has created the world's worst humanitarian crisis, with over 40 violations against journalists and activists documented in May 2023 alone, as stated on en.wikipedia.org).

The international community has taken notice of the crisis, with the US imposing sanctions on an Indian firm, SBL Energy, and its CEO, Alok Choudhari, among eight others, for "fuelling" the civil war in Sudan, as reported by Business Standard. This move is seen as an attempt to curb the flow of weapons and explosives to the warring parties, including the Sudanese Armed Forces and the Rapid Support Forces. The conflict has also drawn comparisons to other global hotspots, such as the situation in North Korea, where Xi Jinping Seeks NKorea Alliance in a bid to expand China's influence in the region. Meanwhile, China Increases Naval Presence in the South China Sea, prompting the US to US Counters China Edge in a bid to maintain regional stability.

The humanitarian crisis in Sudan has reached catastrophic levels, with thousands displaced and hundreds killed in the fighting. The international community is under pressure to act quickly to prevent further escalation, with the UN warning that the situation is rapidly deteriorating. As the conflict continues to unfold, it remains to be seen how the international community will respond to the crisis, and whether the imposition of sanctions will have any significant impact on the warring parties. The situation in Sudan is a stark reminder of the devastating consequences of conflict and the need for urgent international action to prevent further humanitarian disasters.

The Rafael Reyes Take

As I reflect on the escalating crisis in Sudan, I firmly believe that immediate international intervention is necessary to prevent further devastation. The ongoing conflict has resulted in unimaginable human suffering, with thousands displaced and innocent lives lost. If nothing changes, it's clear that the ruling military junta will emerge as the winners, consolidating their power and perpetuating a cycle of violence and oppression. Meanwhile, the Sudanese people will continue to suffer, their voices silenced and their future uncertain. It's imperative that the global community takes action to support a peaceful resolution and hold those in power accountable for their actions.
