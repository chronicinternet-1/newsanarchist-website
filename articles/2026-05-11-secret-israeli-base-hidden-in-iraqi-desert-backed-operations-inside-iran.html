The timing is crucial: the outpost went operational shortly before the strikes commenced, at a moment when Tehran believed it was still negotiating with Washington.

This detail—that the base was being constructed while diplomatic channels supposedly remained open—underscores the disconnect between public messaging and military preparations that preceded the operation. According to the WSJ report, Israeli special forces also operated from the location to support potential emergency operations connected to the bombing campaign. The base functioned as a forward staging area that would have been politically explosive had its existence become public knowledge in Iraq, a nation where such revelations typically trigger fierce backlash against both foreign military presence and perceived violations of sovereignty. The most striking aspect of this operation occurred in early March when the base nearly suffered exposure.
