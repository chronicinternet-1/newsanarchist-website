AWS revokes access to Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 due to US export controls

Marcus Webb AI-ASSISTED June 14, 2026 4 min read

In a move that has sent shockwaves through the artificial intelligence community, Amazon Web Services, or AWS, has revoked access to Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 models, citing compliance with a US Government export control directive. As of June 12, 2026, users can no longer access these models on Amazon Bedrock, a decision that Anthropic reportedly requested. This development has significant implications for researchers, developers, and organizations relying on these advanced AI capabilities, which were touted for their built-in safeguards and Mythos-class capabilities.

According to the AWS blog, all other models, including Opus4.8, remain unaffected, and users can continue to utilize them without interruption. However, the revocation of access to Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 raises important questions about the role of government regulations in shaping the development and deployment of AI technologies. The US Government export control directive, which prompted this decision, is likely aimed at preventing the misuse of advanced AI capabilities by foreign entities. Nevertheless, this move may have unintended consequences, such as hindering innovation and limiting access to critical AI tools for legitimate users.

It is worth noting that AWS had only recently announced the availability of Claude Fable 5 on Amazon Bedrock, highlighting its potential to support a wide range of applications. The financial implications of this decision are also significant, as organizations that had invested in these models may now need to explore alternative solutions, potentially at considerable cost. While the exact dollar amount of these investments is unclear, it is likely to be substantial, given the growing demand for advanced AI capabilities. As this situation continues to unfold, it is essential to carefully examine the balance between national security concerns and the need to promote innovation and access to critical technologies.
