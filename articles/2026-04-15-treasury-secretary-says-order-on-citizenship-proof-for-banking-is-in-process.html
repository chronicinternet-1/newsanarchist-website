This is regulatory theater masquerading as financial discipline. Let's be clear about what's actually happening: we're watching the financialization of citizenship itself, a move that consolidates bank power while appearing to tighten borders. Banks don't need new citizenship verification schemes—they already run Know Your Customer protocols that would make Cold War intelligence agencies blush. What they *want* is explicit government cover to deny banking services to politically inconvenient populations. An executive order provides that shield. The real play here is consolidation. Smaller banks and credit unions, already hemorrhaging to megabanks, will struggle with compliance costs. JPMorgan and Wells Fargo will absorb the friction like swallowing a pebble. De-banking becomes systematic rather than ad-hoc. Bessent knows this. The "in process" language—vague, indefinite—signals they're still calibrating the scope to maximize corporate benefit while minimizing backlash. This isn't about catching terrorists. It's about weaponizing the banking system as a control mechanism, dressed up in patriotic language. Follow the concentration of market power, not the rhetoric.
