Sudan Braces For El Obeid Siege

UN envoy warns of imminent paramilitary assault on El Obeid, threatening thousands of civilians

Rafael Reyes
War correspondent, 11 years covering unreported conflicts
June 26, 2026
4 min read
AI-ASSISTED

Sudan is bracing for a potential siege of the central city of El Obeid, as dozens of countries, including Britain, warn that the paramilitary Rapid Support Forces could imminently escalate an assault, possibly resulting in large-scale atrocities, according to reports from Reuters Sudan News. The United Nations Secretary-General’s Personal Envoy for Sudan has voiced alarm over escalating violence in and around the city of El Obeid, warning that further military escalation could put thousands of civilians at risk and deepen Sudan’s already devastating humanitarian crisis, as reported by UN News. This comes as the Sudanese civil war, which began in 2023, continues to escalate, with the Sudanese Armed Forces and Rapid Support Forces accused of threatening, attacking, and killing journalists and activists, according to Sudanese civil war) information.

The situation in El Obeid is reminiscent of other global conflict zones, where the pursuit of resources, such as oil, has led to devastating consequences, as seen in the Sahel War Escalates article, which highlights the dangers of chasing oil in conflict zones. In Sudan, prosecutors have accused Lundin Oil bosses of being aware of attacks on civilians, but choosing to keep doing business with the government, as reported in the Next Africa newsletter. The prosecution of two executives who did business in Sudan has sparked concerns about the role of international companies in fueling conflict. Meanwhile, the international community is drawing parallels between the situation in Sudan and other global hotspots, such as Taiwan, where the military is simulating attack response drills, and China, where Xi's purge expands to target high-ranking officials.

As the situation in El Obeid continues to deteriorate, the United Nations is warning of a potential paramilitary offensive in the strategic city, which could have far-reaching consequences for the region. The Rapid Support Forces can isolate El Obeid from multiple directions, but sustaining a siege would cost the paramilitary significant manpower, vehicles, and military equipment, according to Sudan researcher Ali Mahmoud Ali. The international community is urging all parties to exercise restraint and work towards a peaceful resolution to the conflict, which has already resulted in significant humanitarian suffering and displacement.
